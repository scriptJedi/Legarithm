What are the requirements to register a company in Cyprus?

Corporate secretary

The law requires that every company has a secretary. This officer is responsible for compliance with legal requirements. He or she files annual returns and resolutions on changes, prepares the annual general meeting, and also deals with the distribution of dividends

Director/nominee director

At least one of your directors must be a resident. Hiring a nominee director is a common and legal practice. A nominee director puts his or her name on your documents but cannot make any key business decisions. In the event of any wrongdoing, such as late or incorrect reporting, the nominee director shares the legal responsibility. That is why you should get an accounting package with this service.

Legal address

All companies must have a local registered address. It is indicated on all legal documents. We receive your correspondence, scan it and forward it to you.

When to submit reports?

The financial statements for the previous year must be submitted to the Companies Register together with the annual return for the current year. Assuming that the company was incorporated on January 1, 2023, you will have 18 months to submit the final annual financial statements for 2023 for the first year of its existence, i.e. the deadline for submission is July 1, 2024

How to file financial statements in Cyprus

Cypriot law provides that a company must file its financial statements with the Registrar of Companies by submitting the documents together with the annual return. When submitting the documents, investors will be required to provide a certificate attesting that the accounts are an exact copy of the accounts presented at the Annual General Meeting (AGM).

It is important to know that the financial statements of a Cyprus company will become available to the general public and can only be submitted if such documents have been approved by the following persons

  • The board of directors
  • The company secretary
  • The general meeting of shareholder

In some cases, such documents must also be verified by an external auditor.

Payment of corporate tax

Final reporting and payment of tax for all company profits (IR4 form) is due two years after the reporting period. In our example, the deadline for submitting the form will be 02/31/2025. In fact, this reporting is a duplicate of the financial statements that your company prepared for the submission of the NE32 form.
